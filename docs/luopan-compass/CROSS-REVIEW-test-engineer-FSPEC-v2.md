| N-02 | **Medium** | §2.5 step 3b, BR-10, §6.1 | **`interference_flag` assignment inconsistency when confidence is Poor but interference is CLEAR.** FSPEC §2.5 step 3b fires the pre-capture warning dialog when "confidence is Poor OR interference_flag is true." ## Positive Observations

- The v0.2-draft is a thorough and well-structured document. All seven Phase 1 FSPEC structural conventions are followed: each flow section has Actors, Preconditions, Behavioral flow, Business rules, and Edge cases.
- The `Clock` interface design in §6.2 is clean and directly usable. `FakeClock.set()` and `FakeClock.advance()` match the patterns used in Phase 1 `FakeTimeSource`. The naming choice of `WallClock` to avoid Android SDK collision is technically sound and worth noting in the TSPEC.
- The `MagneticFieldModel` interface in §6.3 is minimal and correct. Five methods cover all use cases without over-specifying internals. The `getModelId()` method doubles as the `calibration_version` data contract, which elegantly closes the F-03 and the SE `calibration_version` type-confusion finding in a single design decision.
- The `interference_flag` definition in BR-10 and §6.1 correctly decouples interference detection from confidence level — a non-trivial product decision that prevents confusing data in Phase 4 history. The residual N-02 issue is a flow description error, not a design error; the design intent is correct.
- The GRID prevention is defended in depth: toggle constraint, capture logic assertion with exception, business rule, and two distinct acceptance tests (functional + Espresso UI scan). This is exactly the right approach for a "never should happen" programming invariant.
- REQ §8 now includes static WMM2025 test vectors (TE-REQ-02), the FakeClock string format contract for Scenario C, the MagneticFieldModel seam mandate (TE-REQ-09), and the boundary specification for the 30-day cache (Risk P2-R3). These additions to the REQ — likely driven by the prior TE review — make the acceptance criteria directly automatable without requiring a physical device at a known GPS location.

## Recommendation: Approved with Minor Issues

The v0.2-draft resolves all 10 prior High findings and all 9 prior Medium findings (with F-09 and F-15 partially resolved). The document is ready to proceed to TSPEC authoring. The four findings above must be addressed before the PROPERTIES document is finalized:

- **N-01** (High) — Add AT-G-09 for GPS permission denial (maps directly to REQ §8 Scenario G, the seventh scenario currently without an AT).
- **N-02** (Medium) — Fix `interference_flag` assignment in §2.5 step 3b when confidence is Poor but interference is CLEAR; add AT-E-10.
- **N-03** (Medium) — Add encryption correctness AT for bearing DB (AT-PERSIST-01 pattern from Phase 1).
- **N-04** (Medium) — Add force-stop resilience AT for bearing records (AT-PERSIST-02).

N-01 through N-04 do not block TSPEC authoring but MUST be resolved before the PROPERTIES document is approved. N-05 (Low) should be clarified during TSPEC authoring when the Macrobenchmark setup is defined.

The PM may address N-01 through N-04 as a targeted patch to the AT section without re-opening any functional flows. No structural changes to the FSPEC are required.
