| N-01 | High | §6.1 BR-02, normative note | **`calibration_version` type conflict with REQ §5.3.1 is unresolved.** The FSPEC v0.2 has deliberately redefined `calibration_version` as `String` (WMM model name via `MagneticFieldModel.getModelId()`) while the REQ §5.3.1 canonical definition remains `Integer` (`CalibrationRecord.calibration_schema_version`). The normative note in §6.1 says "MUST NOT be conflated" with the schema version integer — but the approved REQ explicitly names the field `calibration_version` with `Integer` type and a schema-version semantic. Until the REQ is updated to either (a) adopt the FSPEC's String/model-name semantic for `calibration_version`, or (b) introduce a separate field name (e.g., `wmm_model_id`) for the model identifier, the two documents remain directly contradictory. Any TSPEC authored from these two documents will require an undocumented judgment call on the field type. | PM must issue a REQ errata for §5.3.1: either update `calibration_version` to `String` with the WMM model-name semantic, or rename the FSPEC field to avoid the collision. | N-02 | Medium | §6.1 | **`lat`/`lon` type (`Double?`) diverges from REQ §5.3.1 (`Float`) without a REQ errata.** The FSPEC provides sound technical reasoning for using `Double` (6 decimal places ≈ 11 cm, required for WMM accuracy), and this reviewer agrees with the reasoning. However, the approved REQ §5.3.1 still specifies `Float` for `latitude` and `longitude`. ## Positive Observations in v0.2-draft

- The interference detection upgrade flow (§2.6) is now clearly and accurately described, with a correct three-tier fallback chain and an explicit note that `GeomagneticField` cannot serve as a no-location fallback. This resolves the highest technical risk from Iteration 1.
- The `MagneticFieldModel` interface in §6.3 is now well-specified: full Kotlin signatures, KDoc comments, a method table, the `epochYears` formula, and explicit acknowledgment that the interface is new. The addition of `getModelId()` and `isExpired()` beyond the REQ minimum is a sensible extension.
- The concurrent-save double-tap debounce (BR-CAP-08) and first-capture consent persistence edge case are new additions that directly address TE findings and are correctly specified.
- The normative notes in §6.1 for `bearing_deg`, `lat`/`lon`, `captured_at`, and `notes` are a significant improvement: each disputed field now carries its own rationale, making the FSPEC's intent unambiguous even where REQ alignment is pending.
- The `WallClock` rename addresses the Android naming collision clearly and the note in §6.2 explains the reasoning. Future engineers will not be confused.

---

## Recommendation: Need Attention

N-01 (`calibration_version` REQ conflict) is a High finding that is a direct, unresolved contradiction between the FSPEC and the approved REQ on the same named field — type, semantics, and value space are all different. TSPEC authoring against these two documents is not possible without an undocumented judgment call. N-05 (`onResume` lifecycle), N-09 (`interference_flag` via `InterferenceState`), N-11 (main-screen cache advisory), N-06 (AT-A-01 measurement), and N-07 (AT-C-02 tolerance) are Medium findings that will produce incorrect or untestable implementations if unaddressed. At minimum, N-01 must be resolved (REQ errata) and N-09 must be corrected before the TSPEC is drafted. The remaining Medium findings (N-02 through N-07, N-11) should be resolved in a v0.3-draft or addressed as REQ errata items before TSPEC sign-off.
